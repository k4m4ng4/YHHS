About the organization

HIV Awareness, Education and approach to Sexual Reproductive Health

  1. Social Behavior Change Communication messages targeting young people between 10 and 29 years old is a priority for YHHS.
  2. Reaching out to adolescents who in school with comprehensive sexuality education (CSE) in partnership with schools as it is part of their curriculums
  3. Through CSE YHHS provide sessions to learners about HIV, condom use, inter-generational sex and gender relations. However, the effects of unbalanced gender relations. Educating the community to discontinue early marriage on young girls
  4. Community/ Religious spaces Youth Reflect Groups, YHHS is using reflect forums strategies to reach out to adolescents who are out of in school.
  5. Provision of free condoms to service delivery points to intensified HIV, STIs, Early pregnancy prevention and educate people to use condoms with sexual partners, especially in multiple concurrent relationships.

Mitigation, Care and Support

Specific activities and strategies;

  1. Provide education support such as school uniforms, shoes and books, pens and other school requirements, (Learning materials).
  2. Promote menstrual health and hygiene which include the production and distribution of reusable sanitary pads to vulnerable girls
  3. Ensure children’s rights, promoting child and youth participation.
  4. Provide Mobile Computer Learning Lab.
  5. Provide child protection and ensure that children are not disadvantaged.
  6. Provide rights-based advocacy programming, raise awareness and social mobilization.
  7. Provision of HIV prevention information and strengthen community structures to respond to orphans and vulnerable children’s needs.
  8. Reduce teenage pregnancy and early child marriage.
  9. Provides child’s ongoing emotional needs, counseling and psychological support to the OVCs and their caregivers/grandmothers, including livelihood and economic strengthening.
  10. Provide stigma and discrimination information.
  11. Strengthen the capacity of families to protect and care for children through family-centered care approach.
  12. Children have access to independent information from a wide range of sources.
  13. Accessible and child-friendly court procedures are in place.
  14. Produce and distribution of reusable and disposable Sanitary Pads for school going girls.
  15. Keeping girls in safe schools including children with special needs.
  16. Disability does NOT discriminate opportunities human rights of anyone.

Community Systems Strengthening

Specific activities and strategies;

  1. YHHS is providing a systematic approach such as working with neighborhood health committees and health centre committees, traditional, cultural and religious leaders for understanding the essential components of community systems and for the design, implementation, monitoring and evaluation of community – based interventions to strengthen these components.
  2. Community System Strengthening has a strong focus on capacity building on human and financial resources (training of community change agents in health, education and village Banking Savings Model).
  3. Enabling community actors such as Chiefs, headmen, Indunas, teachers, Parents, men and women to play a full and effective role alongside health and social welfare systems for health and safe communities. Children protection
  4. Young Happy Healthy & Safe (YHHS) through its Child Safeguarding Policy document in place believes that children are human beings and have rights. YHHS ensure that children go to school as well as learning in a good and safe place, eating well, protecting children from all forms of violence and danger, getting well quickly when the child is unwell, and preventing children from suffering all because they were born in a poor family. The organization ensures that children have the right to participation and protection among other rights and are consulted in decision-making processes concerning their own healthcare, consistent with their evolving capacities. The YHHS organization endeavor to protect children from any form of injustice and harm including child labour, early of forced marriages, gender-based violence and child sexual abuse

Economic Empowerment

Specific activities and strategies;

  1. YHHS provide fertilizers and maize seeds in order to empower grandmothers/caregivers to the OVCs, women and girls, and train them in Village Banking savings group model for economic strengthening.
  2. YHH provide linkages to micro lending institutions.
