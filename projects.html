Projects

SRHR – HIV Knows No Borders

Objectives

• To create demand for SRHR-HIV services by migrants, AYP and SWs in Chipata and Katete migration - affected communities.

• To enhance the Supply and Accessibility of responsive SRHR-HIV services for Migrants, AYP and SWs in Chipata and Katete migration affected communities.

• To create an enabling environment for migrants, AYP and SWs in breaking barriers to address physical, social and economic challenges to health.

Project funded by

The SRHR – HIV Knows No Borders project has provided basis for Sexual Reproductive Health services accessibility, acceptability, appropriateness, effectiveness, and equity for migrants, AYP and SWs in the three districts of Eastern Province being Chipata City, Chadiza and Katete towns realizing the fact that migrants, adolescents, young people and sex workers have unique health needs, and by virtue of their continuous travel, experience difficulty in accessing SRHR – HIV services.

Lack of knowledge on access to SRHR – HIV services hindered their rights to their quality health needs and compromising impact of on-going SRHR – HIV programmes on migrants, adolescents, young people’s health outcomes.

Adolescents and Young People (AYP), Migrants and Sex Workers are on the underserved groups most subjected to abuse and coerced sex. In view of this circumstance, AYP, migrants and SWs face greater health risks, early and unwanted pregnancies, STIs including HIV.

Together we can grow up Happy Healthy and Safe

Objectives

To improve the sexual and reproductive health outcomes and reduce the vulnerabilities of adolescents and youth aged 10-24 through an integrated SRHR/HIV and rights approach in Nyimba district.

To provide a minimum SRHR adolescent and youth health services platform in Nyimba district of Eastern Province of Zambia.

Increase adolescents’ and youths’ awareness, access and utilisation of available SRHR-HIV prevention health services from 13% (average) to 60% to promote healthy living in Nyimba district. and strengthen leadership and governance of an adolescent and youth-responsive health system in 60% of the district.

Project funded by

Young, Happy Healthy and Safe( YHHS) is implementing the project titled, “Together we can grow up happy health and safe” an innovative adolescent and youth prevention project that integrates Comprehensive Sexuality Education, HIV and sexual reproductive health and rights( SRHR) that targets adolescents and young people(girls and boys) both in and out of school aged 10- 24 years in five health facilities namely: Nyimba urban clinic, Chinambi, Mukopeka, Chipembe and Hofmyer rural health centres in Nyimba district of Eastern province. The project provides comprehensive sexuality education (CSE), sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR), reduction of gender-based violence cases, teenage pregnancy, end early and child marriages, provision of condoms to the active adolescents and young people and HIV prevention information and services to the adolescents and young people (young girls and boys) through comprehensive sexuality and life-skills education both in and out of schools and communities. The Together we can grow up happy healthy and safe (HHS) project is implemented in all the five project health facilities/sites funded by the Ministry of Health - Zambia with financial support from Global Fund.

The project contributed in revamping and strengthening the adolescent friendly spaces in the five project sites and this has significantly contributed to the improvement of health service delivery to the adolescents. This has provided a right platform where adolescents are free to discuss matters patterning their sexuality easily. YHHS working hand in hand with the Nyimba District Health Office and through the Adolescent Focal Point persons and the trained peer educators in all the 53 zones continue to find innovative ways to ensure that the environment was conducive around all the friendly spaces.

United National joint programmes on Gender Based Violence phase (II). GRZ-UNDP-GBV phase II

Objectives

GBV Phase II Programme Goal: To contribute to strengthening GBV prevention and response systems in Zambia in line with provisions of the Anti-Gender Based Violence Act.
The Young Happy Healthy and Safe is currently implementing a United Nations Joint Programme on Gender-Based Violence GRZ-UNDP-GBV Phase II supported directly by the United Nations Development Programme, Zambia Country Office. The Joint GRZ-UNDP-GBV Phase II is a multi-partner programme designed to reduce the prevalence of Gender Based Violence in Zambia.
The programme targets the women, girls, boys, men, and GBV survivors, those who are marginalized such as people with disabilities. The programme also integrates COVID-19 in all its GBV programming and implements its activities in communities, chiefdoms, schools and work places. The Joint GRZ-UNDP-GBV Phase II addresses cultural and traditional norms that fuel the Gender-Based Violence and YHHS work with the traditional leaders and Village-Led One Stop Centre (VLOSC).

SRHR – HIV KNBs Programme

Approach

The SRHR – HIV KNB programme enhances:

1. The demand side for SRH and HIV information and services;
2. The supply side of youth friendly SRH – HIV services and skills training;
3. The enabling environment generating more respect for the SRHR of YVP and facilitate policy reforms and implementation of regional and national policy agreements to the local context.

Objectives of the project;

Improve SRH and HIV outcomes and quality of life among young vulnerable people (YVP) aged 10 to 29 years including migrants and sex workers living in migration affected communities in three districts being Chipata Katete and Vubwi in Eastern Province .

Healthy Choice:

Young vulnerable people (YVP), including migrants and sex workers living in migration affected communities have safe sexual behavior and greater freedom of choice about their HIV and SRH rights by December 2026.

Access to services

Young Vulnerable People (YVP), including migrants and sex workers living in migration affected communities have increased access to and utilization of quality SRH – HIV and other support services by December 2026

Creating an enabling environment

Young Vulnerable People (YVP), including migrants and sex workers living in migration affected communities in three districts being Chipata, Katete and Vubwi in Eastern Province have their SRH – HIV rights and needs progressively addressed in changed socio – cultural norms, policy reforms and implementation at local, national and regional levels by December 2026
