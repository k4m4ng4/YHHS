SRHR – HIV Knows No Borders

Introduction

YHHS is an implementing partner for multiple donor projects in the 8 districts of Eastern Province among them Save the Children International. The province comprises of 14 districts in total. This translate that YHHS covers well over 50% of the Province with adolescents in SRHR-HIV interventions to explore the possibility of making young people’s current health services accessible, this includes SRH, and systems for Adolescents responsive


Goal

The overall goal is to improve the health status of adolescents in Eastern Province and in Zambia as a whole.
Save the Children International, Zambia Country Office partnered with Young Happy Health and Safe in 2017 to date in implementing a regional programme called SRHR-HIV Knows No Borders (KNB) Programme which is implemented in six countries in Southern Africa region (Lesotho, the Kingdom of Eswatini, Zambia, Malawi, Mozambique and Republic of South Africa with support from the Netherlands Government.
In KNB collaborative project, YHHS implements SRHR-HIV which seeks to improve access to SRHR and HIV services and information to the least served migrants and migration-affected communities along cross-border and migration corridors in the region. YHHS through KNB project contributes to improve health outcomes among migrants, Adolescents and Young people (AYPs) and Sex workers (SWs) and migration-affected communities by promoting positive healthy sexual relationships that can contribute to reducing new HIV infections and improving Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR) indicators by reducing prevalence of sexually transmitted infections (STI), teenage pregnancy, maternal mortality rate (MMR), unmet need for family planning (FP), increasing the use of modern contraception and greater freedom of choice about sexuality.


YHHS ADH Objectives

1. To ensure availability of the minimum adolescent health services for young people in all the districts of Eastern Province in Zambia by 2026
2. Increase adolescents’ awareness and utilization of available health services to promote healthy living of young vulnerable people, migrants and sex workers.

Community Care and Support

Introduction

The Project seeks to reinforce community bonds by providing support for OVC in their own communities. The Project will continue to implement its “Whole School Site” model in which school management and students will actively participate in the creation of an enabling environment for OVCs in schools. Embedded in the daily activities and ethos of each school, the Project will foster SRH and HIV prevention education, enforcement of child protection policies, girls’ retention in school via protective structures and psychosocial support. The Project will continue to implement activities with the tablets in the Mobile Learning Lab (Rachel-Plus), improving access to quality education for girls in remove rural communities where electricity is erratic and internet access is undependable. The major goal of this year is to provide basic health care and material support to OVCs while supporting them to get an education.

Overall objective:

To empower, strengthen and sustain community capacity to provide care and support for OVC, caregivers and PLWHA.


Specific objectives:

1. Increase awareness of HIV & AIDS, to ensure that OVC and households affected by and vulnerable to HIV & AIDS have access holistic psychosocial support, SRH services and quality HIV prevention, care and support.
2. To provide OVC and their households with education, psychosocial, socio-economic and nutritional support.
3. To develop new initiatives and support to existing efforts designed to strengthen the capacity of families and communities providing prevention, care and support services to children affected and or infected by HIV & AIDS

Together we can grow up Happy Healthy and Safe

introduction

Young, Happy Healthy and Safe( YHHS) is implementing the project titled, “Together we can grow up happy health and safe” an innovative adolescent and youth prevention project that integrates Comprehensive Sexuality Education, HIV and sexual reproductive health and rights( SRHR) that targets adolescents and young people(girls and boys) both in and out of school aged 10- 24 years in five health facilities namely: Nyimba urban clinic, Chinambi, Mukopeka, Chipembe and Hofmyer rural health centres in Nyimba district of Eastern province. The project provides comprehensive sexuality education (CSE), sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR), reduction of gender-based violence cases, teenage pregnancy, end early and child marriages, provision of condoms to the active adolescents and young people and HIV prevention information and services to the adolescents and young people (young girls and boys) through comprehensive sexuality and life-skills education both in and out of schools and communities. The Together we can grow up happy healthy and safe (HHS) project is implemented in all the five project health facilities/sites funded by the Ministry of Health - Zambia with financial support from Global Fund.

Objectives

To improve the sexual and reproductive health outcomes and reduce the vulnerabilities of adolescents and youth aged 10-24 through an integrated SRHR/HIV and rights approach in Nyimba district.

To provide a minimum SRHR adolescent and youth health services platform in Nyimba district of Eastern Province of Zambia.

Increase adolescents’ and youths’ awareness, access and utilisation of available SRHR-HIV prevention health services from 13% (average) to 60% to promote healthy living in Nyimba district. and strengthen leadership and governance of an adolescent and youth-responsive health system in 60% of the district.

Results

The project contributed in revamping and strengthening the adolescent friendly spaces in the five project sites and this has significantly contributed to the improvement of health service delivery to the adolescents. This has provided a right platform where adolescents are free to discuss matters patterning their sexuality easily. YHHS working hand in hand with the Nyimba District Health Office and through the Adolescent Focal Point persons and the trained peer educators in all the 53 zones continue to find innovative ways to ensure that the environment was conducive around all the friendly spaces.

To challenge violence against women and girls; Rape, sexual and physical violence, forced marriage, wife battering, denial of opportunities and psychological violence are some of the most common forms of gender based violence in Chipata. Breakfree!

Introduction

With Funding from SRHR Africa Trust, YHHS is implementing the Break Free project in Eastern Province of Zambia with focus in Chipata and Chipangali. The project has a primary focus on 3 outcomes which are Adolescents access to quality SRHR information, education and services. In view of the foregoing, the organization is calling for External Consultancy Services to contribute towards effectively implementation of the project. It’s a 5 year project. 2021 – 2025. The project aims to reach children and adolescents aged 5-18 years with SRHR information and Comprehensive Sexuality Education, prevention of Teen Pregnancies, Child Marriage and promoting increased access to SRHR services. Secondary target groups include young people aged 19-24 years, with a focus on parents, teachers, community health providers, and community leadership including traditional and religious leaders and national governments. At regional level the project is targeting regional bodies with member state representation (SADC, EAC, and AU).The implementation of the project has been made possible through the support of the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs through Plan Netherlands.

Purpose of the Inter-generational Dialogue meeting

The Provincial Dialogue meeting is a platform to share experiences in the existing interventions on Child Marriage and associated effects. The event will also bring to the fore how access to SRHR services can help bridge the gaps to high Teen Pregnancies, Sexual Gender Based Violence and ultimately improve the health outcomes of adolescents and young people in the province. Further, this event will be used as an opportunity to meaningfully learn and engage on how to effectively scale up SRHR programmes for children, adolescents and young people (CAYPs) under the Break Free project.


Objectives of the Inter-Generational Dialogue Workshop

• Share the Break Free project focus to stimulate effective engagement of traditional, religious leaders and strategic government line ministries and other key stakeholders from the onset.
• Facilitate sharing of experiences and success stories in addressing Teen Pregnancy, Child Marriage and SRHR provision in the province so as to effectively scale up SRHR programs for children and adolescents in the province.
• Enhance stakeholders and leaderships’ understanding of current challenges and opportunities for integration of Child Marriage, Teen Pregnancy and SRHR programs in the respective district and provincial plans as a way of building synergies and sustainability.
• Develop common understanding and actionable interventions among participants
